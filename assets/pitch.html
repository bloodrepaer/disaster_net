THE PROBLEM

When disaster hits,
four things break.

Relief organizations arrive to find the same four failures every single time. DisasterNet was designed from the ground up to solve all of them — including the internet going down first.

01
No coordination
Rescue teams operate in silos. No one has a live picture of which zones are critical, which convoys are blocked, which hospitals are overflowing.

ZAMA TFHE · LIT PROTOCOL
Medical data that's private even from us

Missing persons and medical records are encrypted with Fully Homomorphic Encryption — Zama TFHE — before they ever leave the device. The encrypted ciphertext is what gets broadcast to the mesh.

Only physicians with a verified credential can decrypt via Lit Protocol key gate. Not the platform. Not the volunteers. Not the government. The math enforces the privacy, not a promise.

STARKNET · NEAR · HYPERCERTS
Aid that can't be stolen

Donor funds sit in a Starknet smart contract. They release to NGOs only when field conditions are verified. No middlemen. No discretion. The contract enforces the rules.

Every volunteer who helps gets a Hypercert — an ERC-8004 on-chain contribution certificate. Their identity is anchored on NEAR Protocol via World ID proof-of-personhood. Permanent, portable proof of humanitarian work.

HACKATHON TRACKS

Four tracks.
One unified system.

DisasterNet doesn't stretch to fit tracks — every track is a load-bearing piece of the architecture.

🏅
Funding the Commons
Hypercerts mint contribution certificates for every verified rescue action. Volunteers accumulate portable proof of humanitarian work. Donors can match contributions on-chain. DisasterNet turns relief work into a public good with verifiable impact accounting.
🔒
Privacy
Zama TFHE encrypts medical records before they leave the device. Lit Protocol enforces physician-only key access. Not even DisasterNet can read the data. The cryptography enforces the privacy — not a terms of service.
⚡
AI
Impulse AI is a Claude-powered autonomous triage agent. It reads plain-language field reports and issues specific routing decisions, resource reallocations, and escalation triggers — all with IPFS-backed provenance and Hypercert accountability.
🌐
Infrastructure
Offline-first BroadcastChannel mesh. IPFS + Filecoin via Storacha for permanent decentralized storage. Starknet for trustless fund disbursement. NEAR Protocol for identity anchoring. Everything works when the internet doesn't.
